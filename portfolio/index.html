Data Science Projects

Applied ML and econometric research from ECON 3916: Data Science for Economists.

01

Replicating the NY Fed Yield Curve Recession Model

Replicated the Federal Reserve Bank of New York's recession probability model using FRED data. Demonstrated LPM failure on binary outcomes, then fit logistic regression on the 10Y–3M Treasury spread lagged 12 months to forecast NBER recession months. The model captured every recession in-sample, but the 2022–24 inversion — the longest since the 1980s — produced elevated recession probabilities with no recession materializing, exposing the limits of single-predictor leading-indicator models.

02

Clustering World Economies with K-Means & PCA

Collected 10 World Bank development indicators for ~160 countries via the wbgapi API, standardized features, and applied K-Means clustering with K=4 — validated by elbow and silhouette analysis. PCA reduced the space to 2D for visualization. Clusters revealed a dense core of similar mid-income countries, with smaller groups separating along unemployment and digital connectivity, challenging traditional GDP-based income classifications.

scikit-learnwbgapipandasmatplotlib
GitHub ↗

03

Fraud Detection: Metrics Beyond Accuracy

Evaluated logistic regression on 284,807 credit card transactions with a 0.172% fraud rate. Demonstrated the accuracy paradox, optimized classification thresholds via Precision-Recall curves, and incorporated a real-world constraint of 500 daily investigator reviews.

scikit-learnpandasmatplotlib
GitHub ↗

04

Causal ML: 401(k) Policy Evaluation

Estimated the causal effect of 401(k) eligibility on household net financial assets using Double Machine Learning. Random Forest nuisance learners with 5-fold cross-fitting separated prediction from causal inference. Conducted CATE analysis by income quartile and sensitivity analysis for unobserved confounding.
